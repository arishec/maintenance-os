## PRODUCT HUNT

**Name:** Maintenance OS

**Tagline (60 chars max):**
Stop chasing contractors. Manage repairs in one place.

**Description:**
Most landlords and homeowners don't have a repair system — they have scattered texts, missed calls, and quotes buried in email threads.

Maintenance OS replaces all of that with one workflow: a tenant or homeowner submits an issue, you dispatch it to multiple contractors at once, compare quotes side by side, track the job from start to finish, and keep a searchable repair history.

No more chasing contractors for responses. No more forgetting who fixed what. No more spreadsheets pretending to be a system.

**Key features:**
- Structured intake forms (photos, urgency, details — submitted once)
- One-click dispatch to multiple contractors via SMS and email
- Side-by-side quote comparison (price, timeline, scope)
- Job tracking from scheduled to completed
- Full repair history searchable by property, contractor, or date

**Who it's for:**
Homeowners and landlords managing 1–50+ properties who are tired of managing repairs through conversations.

**Pricing:**
Free during beta. No credit card required.

**Website:** https://ifbids.com

## INDIEHACKERS

**Post title:**
I built a repair management system for landlords — here's what I learned

**Post body:**

I've been managing rental properties and the #1 pain point was always the same: repairs are chaos.

A tenant texts you about a leak. You call three plumbers. One responds by text, one by email, one doesn't respond at all. You pick the cheapest, forget to tell the tenant, and six months later you can't remember who did the work or what it cost.

I built Maintenance OS to fix this. Here's how it works:

1. **Intake** — Tenant submits the issue once with photos, details, and urgency
2. **Dispatch** — You send the job to multiple contractors with one click
3. **Compare** — Quotes come back into one view: price, timeline, scope
4. **Track** — Follow the job from scheduled to completed
5. **History** — Every repair is saved and searchable

The whole point is: one request in, one workflow out, nothing falls through the cracks.
